Last week I went all-in building with my Clawdbot / OpenClaw. This morning, I was burnt out, and questioning everything. Here's what happened — and what I learned.

<!-- more -->

It started innocently. I set up Clawdbot with a workspace, connected it to WhatsApp, and was blown away by how much power that unlocked. Simple use cases first — reminders, news digests, the usual.

Then things escalated. I started vibe coding. Gave it developer access to my personal repo project, hooked it up to the cloud to deploy and debug. Suddenly I wasn't just playing around — I was building real things, fast.

Every spare moment — and I mean every — went into building. Waiting in line? Building. Lunch break? Building. The excitement was intoxicating.

Then came the guilt. Any minute not spent building felt wasted. I used to do 10K runs every morning. I stopped. Building felt more important.

Late nights. Early mornings. Days blurred together. Tokens ran out on Claude? I switched to Kimi. Maxed that out? Minimax. I just couldn't stop.

Scrolling X made it worse. So much happening, so many people shipping. FOMO hit hard. The anxiety crept in. By the end of the week, I caught an actual cold. My body said what my mind wouldn't: slow down.

But here's the thing — getting sick gave me something I hadn't allowed myself all week: time to stop and think.

I asked myself: what's the purpose of all this? The answer was clear — to become better and to build things that help others. Not to burn out chasing every shiny tool.

So I'm setting boundaries now:

- **Define when to work and when to rest.**
- **Accept I can't keep up with everything.**
- **Be purposeful and mindful, not reactive and frantic.**

This feels like an adaptation phase. Something genuinely exciting and enabling landed in our hands, and it's natural to go overboard at first.

But **sustainable and mindful > intense**.
